About Mental Health

Mental health is a state of mental well-being that enables people to cope with the stresses of life, realize their abilities, learn well and work well, and contribute to their community. It has intrinsic and instrumental value and is integral to our well-being.

At any one time, a diverse set of individual, family, community and structural factors may combine to protect or undermine mental health. Although most people are resilient, people who are exposed to adverse circumstances – including poverty, violence, disability and inequality – are at higher risk of developing a mental health condition.

Many mental health conditions can be effectively treated at relatively low cost, yet health systems remain significantly under-resourced and treatment gaps are wide all over the world. Mental health care is often poor in quality when delivered. People with mental health conditions often also experience stigma, discrimination and human rights violations. Despite its importance, stigma and lack of awareness still hinder access to care. Our mission is to create a safe and supportive environment where everyone can thrive.

Inspiring Stories

John's Story: Finding Strength in Vulnerability

“Therapy taught me that seeking help is a strength, not a weakness.”

John was a high-achieving student who always seemed to have it all together. But behind closed doors, he battled severe anxiety that made even the simplest tasks overwhelming. For years, he kept his struggles hidden, fearing judgment from others.

One day, after a panic attack left him unable to leave his room, John decided to seek help. With the support of a therapist, he began to understand the root of his anxiety and learned techniques to manage it. Through therapy, he realized that asking for help wasn’t a sign of failure but a brave step toward healing.

Now, John openly shares his journey, encouraging others to prioritize their mental health. He has become an advocate for therapy and mental health education, inspiring those around him to seek support without fear.

Emma's Journey: Turning Pain into Purpose

“Becoming an advocate gave me purpose and hope for the future.”

Emma grew up in a family that believed mental health issues were something to "snap out of." Struggling with depression during her teenage years, she often felt isolated and misunderstood. Her lowest point came during her first year of college when she thought she couldn’t go on.

A compassionate professor noticed the signs and referred her to the university’s counseling center. The sessions were life-changing. With professional guidance and support, Emma began to rebuild her self-esteem and rediscover her passions.

Today, Emma is a mental health advocate who speaks at schools and community events. She uses her story to break the stigma surrounding mental illness, showing that recovery is possible and that no one should suffer in silence.

Raj's Experience: The Power of Community Support

“Community support was the lifeline I didn’t know I needed.”

Raj had always been the "life of the party," but beneath his cheerful exterior, he struggled with bipolar disorder. The highs and lows took a toll on his relationships and career, leaving him feeling isolated and hopeless.

One evening, while browsing online, he stumbled upon a local mental health support group. Nervous but desperate for connection, Raj decided to attend a meeting. What he found was a safe space where he could share his experiences without fear of judgment.

The support group became a lifeline for Raj, helping him understand that he wasn’t alone in his struggles. With encouragement from his peers, he sought professional treatment and developed strategies to manage his condition. Raj now leads the same support group that changed his life, offering hope and guidance to others navigating their own mental health journeys.

Simone's Experience: Overcoming the Pressure and Prioritizing Mental Health

“I knew that I had to put my mental health first. It was a tough decision, but one I don't regret.”

Simone Biles, one of the most decorated gymnasts in history, made headlines during the 2020 Tokyo Olympics when she withdrew from several events to focus on her mental health.

Simone faced a mental health challenge known as the "twisties," a dangerous condition where gymnasts lose spatial awareness mid-air. This condition is a serious risk in a sport that requires immense precision. By stepping back, Simone not only protected herself but also started an important conversation about the need for athletes—and people everywhere—to prioritize their mental health over external pressures. Her story has empowered others, especially those in high-stress professions, to take their mental health seriously and seek help when needed.

Carrie's Journey: Battling Bipolar Disorder and Advocating for Mental Health

“I don't want to be somebody who's pretending to be perfect. I want to be somebody who's honest about their struggle.”

Carrie openly discussed her struggles with bipolar disorder, addiction, and the challenges she faced while navigating the entertainment industry.

She became a champion for mental health, using her platform to share her story and break down the stigma surrounding mental illness. She wrote about her experiences in her memoir Wishful Drinking, where she candidly shared her battle with mental health and the importance of seeking help. Carrie’s transparency inspired millions of people to understand that mental health issues are not a sign of weakness but a part of being human. Her legacy continues to encourage others to speak openly about their struggles and seek the help they need.

Dwayne's Story: Breaking the Silence on Depression

“I found that with depression, one of the most important things you can realize is that you’re not alone.”

In interviews, he has shared that, despite his success and fame, he faced severe depression during his teenage years and later in life. At one point, he even contemplated suicide after a series of challenging life events.

What sets Dwayne's story apart is his willingness to speak publicly about his struggles with mental health. He has become an advocate for men’s mental health, encouraging people, especially men, to seek help when they need it. His transparency about his experiences has helped to dismantle the stigma surrounding mental health, particularly within communities that traditionally view asking for help as a sign of weakness. Dwayne’s story has inspired countless individuals to recognize their struggles, seek support, and not be afraid to talk about mental health.

Summary of Inspiring Stories

These inspiring stories highlight the power of vulnerability, seeking help, and prioritizing mental health. John, Emma, and Raj each faced significant mental health challenges—anxiety, depression, and bipolar disorder—and found strength in reaching out for support. Their journeys reflect the importance of therapy, community support, and breaking the stigma surrounding mental health struggles. Similarly, high-profile figures like Simone Biles, Carrie Fisher, and Dwayne "The Rock" Johnson have also shared their personal experiences with mental health, using their platforms to inspire others. Simone Biles' decision to step back from the Olympics to protect her mental health, Carrie Fisher’s advocacy for mental illness awareness, and Dwayne Johnson’s openness about his depression all serve as powerful reminders that mental health is a crucial part of overall well-being. These stories encourage others to seek help, speak openly about their struggles, and prioritize their mental health, showing that strength lies in vulnerability and seeking support when needed.
